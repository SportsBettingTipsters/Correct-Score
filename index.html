What is Correct Score in Betting?

Correct Score betting is a football market where you predict the exact final score of a match at the end of full-time. This requires not only selecting the winning outcome but also accurately predicting the number of goals scored by each team.

For a correct score example:

  • Correct Score Winning Bet: Predicting 2-1 as the final score, and the match ends 2-1.
  • Correct Score Losing Bet: Predicting 1-0, but the match ends 2-0 or 1-1.

This market offers some of the highest odds in football betting due to its difficulty, appealing to experienced punters who enjoy the challenge of precise predictions and the potential for significant returns.

Does Correct Score Betting Include Extra Time?

Correct Score betting applies only to the full-time result of a football match, including stoppage time but excluding extra time and penalty shootouts. This means your prediction must match the scoreline at the end of the standard 90 minutes of play plus any additional stoppage time added by the referee.

For example, if you bet on a 2-1 Correct Score and the match ends 2-1 after 90 minutes and stoppage time, your bet wins—even if the game goes into extra time or penalties with a different final score. However, if the match ends 1-1 at full time and a winner emerges in extra time or penalties, your bet will lose.

It’s important to check the specific market rules, but in most cases, Correct Score bets are strictly settled based on the standard 90-minute result.

What Is the Best Correct Score Betting Strategy?

A successful Correct Score strategy focuses on analysing match dynamics and key statistical trends. Here are four essential factors to consider:

  • Team Form and Defence: Analyse recent performances, including goals scored and conceded. Low-scoring teams often produce 1-0 or 1-1 results, while attacking teams create higher scorelines.
  • Head-to-Head History: Review previous encounters between the teams. Recurring scorelines can highlight patterns and inform your predictions.
  • Goal Timing: Evaluate the average time teams score or concede goals. Matches with late scorers may skew towards tighter results.
  • League Trends: Some leagues favour specific scorelines due to playing styles. For example, Serie A often produces 1-0 or 2-1 results. Using expert analysis will help you understand the patterns.

Combining these insights helps you create more informed and accurate scoreline predictions.
